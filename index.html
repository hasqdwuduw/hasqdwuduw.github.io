Overview of LLMs

Large Language Models (LLMs), such as the Generative Pre-trained Transformer (GPT) series, are among the most exciting
developments in current artificial intelligence research. By leveraging deep learning techniques, LLMs can comprehend,
predict, and generate natural language.

This inspires the idea of applying LLMs in the field of medicine.

Prospects of LLMs in Clinic

In the field of clinic, the potential of LLMs is gradually being recognized and explored. Some possible applications include:

Medical Record Analysis and Generation: Automatically analyze patient information and generate medical record reports.

Medical Literature Retrieval and Analysis: Efficiently retrieve medical literature and provide key information summaries.

Patient Communication: Engage in preliminary natural language communication with patients, providing health consultations.

Assistance in Diagnostic Support: Analyze clinical records and provide preliminary diagnostic suggestions.

These application scenarios only scratch the surface of the capabilities of LLMs. With technological advancements and
the increasing availability of medical data, we can expect LLMs to play an increasingly important role in future clinic field.

Getting started

Here are some datasets in the domain of clinic which can be used to train, fine-tune or evaluate your model.

· clinical instruction data: Some datasets including data of the real conversations
between patients and doctors.

· MedMCQA: It is a large-scale multi-Subject multi-choice dataset for medical domain Question Answering task.

· Chinese medical dialogue data: A Chinese medical dialogue data about various aspects.

My experiment

I fine-tune the TinyLLaMA-1b on clinical instruction dataset
and evaluate it on the MedMCQA dataset to verify its performance. With training only one epoch on training set, It improves the
accuracy from 24.3% to 31.2%.

Future work can be done on using larger models and training models with more data to validate the practicality of using LLMs to
communicate with patients and give them some medical suggestions.
